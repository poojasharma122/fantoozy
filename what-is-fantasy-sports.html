What Is Fantasy Sports?

Fantasy sports let fans step into the role of team manager. Instead of just watching games, you draft real-life athletes onto a virtual roster. Their real-world performances translate into fantasy points, and your team competes against others in your league.

Most fantasy leagues run across an entire season, but Daily Fantasy Sports (DFS) condense the experience into one day or one week of competition. The main appeal is control: you decide who to draft, when to trade, and how to manage lineups.

  • Season-Long Leagues: Draft once and manage all year.
  • DFS (Daily Fantasy Sports): Draft new teams each day for quicker action.
  • Head-to-Head: Compete against one opponent per week.
  • Rotisserie (Roto): Standings based on cumulative stats across the season.
