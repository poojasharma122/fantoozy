Drafting in NHL fantasy is different from other sports. With smaller rosters, multi-position players, and unpredictable injuries, the draft requires a sharp balance.

Draft-Day Strategies

  • Prioritize Goalies Early: Goaltenders carry enormous value in fantasy. Drafting at least one elite starter can stabilize your roster.
  • Stack Offensive Lines: Drafting teammates who play on the same line or power play (line stacking) creates bonus points when they combine for goals and assists.
  • Target Dual-Position Players: Players eligible at multiple positions (C/LW or LW/RW) give lineup flexibility all season.
  • Draft Depth on Defense: Defensemen who play on the power play often produce at near-forward levels.
  • Balance Scoring & Grit Stats: Leagues that count hits, blocks, and penalty minutes reward physical players as much as scorers.
